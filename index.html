01. The Beginning
There from day one.

FindUrNemo has been in the NFT space since 2021, back when the concept of digital ownership on a blockchain was still a radical new idea. He was not someone who arrived after the hype. He was part of the community before most people had even heard the word NFT. That history matters. He has seen every cycle, every wave of new collectors, every shift in the market, and through all of it he kept building.

The idea for CryptoFish crystallized in 2022. He had long been fascinated by ichthyology, the scientific study of fish, and he saw the blockchain as a once-in-a-generation opportunity. Not to flip JPEGs, but to create a permanent, on-chain archive of the world's fish species, with real scientific taxonomy built into every token. The soft mint launched in 2022 and the work that followed would consume years of his life.

From that starting point in 2022, FindUrNemo took on something that no single person had attempted at this scale: hand-illustrating over 2,166 individual fish species, each one researched, classified, and drawn from scratch. No generators. No templates. No shortcuts.

02. The Pixel Art
Making something real out of something tiny.

Every CryptoFish is hand-drawn in pixel art. What sounds simple is in practice a profound creative and technical challenge. Pixel art imposes extreme constraints. You are working within a grid of limited resolution, where every single pixel placement is a deliberate decision. There is no brushstroke you can blur. There is no layer mask to hide an error. Every mark either works or it does not.

The challenge FindUrNemo set for himself was not just to draw a fish in pixels, but to make it recognizable. Each species has a distinct silhouette, a characteristic fin structure, a specific coloration pattern that sets it apart from every other fish in the world. A Tropheus moorii from Kambwimba looks subtly but definitively different from a Tropheus moorii from Linangu. Getting those distinctions right within a constrained pixel canvas, while still making each fish look like its real-world counterpart, required a level of observational skill that compounds with every single entry in the collection.

Across 2,166 fish and counting, the cumulative time investment is staggering. Each token represents hours of work: research, reference study, taxonomy confirmation, then the actual drawing. That work happened quietly, consistently, over years. Most collectors who hold a CryptoFish have no idea how much of a human life is embedded in that image.

03. The Research
Every fish took hours before a single pixel was placed.

Before FindUrNemo draws anything, he researches. Each fish in the collection is built on real taxonomy: order, family, genus, and species, sourced against FishBase and the IUCN Red List. The locality is specific, not just to a region but to the exact body of water where the species is found. That detail is embedded directly in the token metadata.

The conservation status for each fish comes directly from the IUCN Red List. If a species is Critically Endangered, the token reflects that. The rarity in the collection is not manufactured. It mirrors what is actually happening to these animals in the wild.

04. The 150+ Customs
He gave away over 150 hand-crafted tokens.

One of the most remarkable and undersold aspects of CryptoFish is what FindUrNemo did for his community. Over the course of the project, he personally hand-drew and minted more than 150 custom and honorary tokens, and then sent them out to fellow collectors, artists, community figures, and friends, at no charge. Each one is a unique, original piece of pixel art. Each one took real time and skill to produce. And he gave them away.

These are not derivative works or quick sketches. They are full CryptoFish tokens, created with the same care and precision as every other entry in the collection. Some incorporate inside jokes or references meaningful to that specific person. Every single one is a one-of-one gift that required FindUrNemo to sit down, research a concept, draw it pixel by pixel, mint it on-chain, and send it out. Over 150 times.

That level of generosity toward a community is rare in any creative space. In the NFT world, where most projects treat their holders as liquidity, it is almost unheard of. It speaks to who FindUrNemo actually is: someone who was in this for the love of the craft and the love of the community, long before anyone was paying attention.

05. The Why
Preservation first. Archival by design.

Most NFT collections treat rarity as a game mechanic: a rare hat, a background color, distributed by an algorithm to manufacture artificial scarcity. CryptoFish does the opposite. Rarity is inherited directly from nature. A Critically Endangered species is rare in the collection because it is critically endangered in the world. The tokens know this. The metadata knows this. The blockchain preserves it permanently.

FindUrNemo's goal was always an archive: a permanent record of the world's fish species, with their full scientific identity intact, living on-chain in a form that can outlast any platform, marketplace, or trend. That goal required not just artistic skill but intellectual commitment and patience that most people could not sustain. He sustained it across years, across 2,166 tokens and counting, without losing the standard he set for himself on day one.

What FindUrNemo built is genuinely rare in the history of digital art. Not rare in the sense of limited supply. Rare in the sense that almost no one would have had the knowledge, the skill, the patience, and the dedication to create something like this. The fact that it exists at all is a testament to one person deciding to do something extraordinary and seeing it through.
